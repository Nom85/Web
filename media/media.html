Madeira Cetacean Fieldwork 2021

More than 20 boat-based surveys supporting cetacean monitoring, photo-identification and marine biotechnology projects.

During my time in Madeira, I participated in more than twenty field expeditions focused on cetacean monitoring and marine biotechnology research.

The work combined boat-based surveys, photo-identification campaigns, biological sampling and laboratory support activities. These expeditions provided a unique opportunity to understand how scientific fieldwork operates in real marine environments where weather, logistics and timing continuously shape decision-making.

Beyond data collection, the experience strengthened my understanding of teamwork, operational planning and the connection between marine conservation and applied research.

Tjärnö Bay Research Expedition

Ten-day field project exploring benthic biodiversity across natural and artificial habitats in Sweden.

This ten-day expedition focused on understanding how benthic communities differ between natural and artificial habitats in Tjärnö Bay.

The project involved field sampling, species identification, navigation to sampling stations, risk assessments, data analysis and scientific reporting.

Working in a multidisciplinary environment required balancing field logistics, laboratory processing and scientific objectives while adapting to changing conditions.

The expedition culminated in a scientific presentation and a short documentary video summarizing the project and its findings.
